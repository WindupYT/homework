Legislation

Legislation in the field of computer science refers to the body of laws and regulations that govern the use, development, and protection of technology, data, and computer systems. These laws focus on a number of issues, such as cybercrime, privacy, and security, all of which are very important in determining the legal framework surrounding technology.

There are the four recognised laws, which are involved in governing the protection of data and computer systems:

  1. The Data Protection act 1998
  2. The Computer Misuse Act 1990
  3. The Copyright Design and Patents Act 1988
  4. The regulation of Investigatory Powers
